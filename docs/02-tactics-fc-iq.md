# 02 — Tactics (FC IQ) & Team Strategies

FC 26 uses the **FC IQ** system: instead of micromanaging "work rates" and sliders,
you set a **team-wide tactical plan** (how you build up, how you defend, where your
lines sit) and assign each player a **Role** with a **Focus** (see `04`). The team
plan + roles together decide off-ball movement and shape.

You get multiple **custom tactics slots** plus in-game **tactical presets** you can
swap on the D-pad mid-match. Set them up *before* kickoff.

---

## The four pillars of a team plan

### 1. Build-Up Style (in possession)
- **Balanced** — safe default; mix of short and direct.
- **Counter** — players break forward fast on the turnover. Pairs with pace up top
  and a deep block. Best low-effort win-condition for most players.
- **Short Passing / Possession** — players offer more support, shorter angles.
  Needs technical midfielders and patience; punishes loose touches.
- **Long Ball / Direct** — quick service to a target striker; great vs a high line,
  ugly but effective.

### 2. Defensive Approach (out of possession)
- **Deep** — bank of defenders sits, protects the box, invites pressure. Safest;
  best vs better opponents and vs pace.
- **Balanced** — standard mid-block.
- **High / Aggressive Press** — squeeze high, win the ball early. High reward, but
  one through ball = a 1v1. Only run it with **pace at CB** and the stamina to
  sustain it; turn it OFF when leading or tired.

### 3. Defensive Width & Depth (the lines)
- **Width:** narrow to protect the half-spaces and central overloads; wide to
  defend the touchlines and stop crossers. Default slightly **narrow** — most goals
  come through the middle.
- **Depth (line height):** lower = safer vs pace; higher = compresses space but
  exposes the channel behind. **Drop your line when ahead.**

### 4. Tempo / Player Roles
The team plan only comes alive once roles are assigned (see `04`). Roles decide who
stays, who joins, who underlaps.

## Five plug-and-play strategies

Each lists: identity → build-up → defense → which formation (see `03`) → win
condition.

### A. "Patient Possession" (control the game)
- **Build-up:** Short Passing / Possession · **Defense:** Balanced, narrow,
  mid line.
- **Formation:** `4-3-3 (False 9)` or `4-2-3-1`.
- **Key roles:** a **Deep-Lying Playmaker** pivot + **Falseback** fullbacks that
  tuck in to give passing triangles.
- **Win condition:** starve the opponent of the ball, wait for them to over-press,
  then play through the lines with an Incisive Pass.

### B. "Lethal Counter" (lowest effort, very effective)
- **Build-up:** Counter · **Defense:** Deep, narrow, low line.
- **Formation:** `4-2-3-1` or `4-4-2`.
- **Key roles:** **two Holding pivots**, an **Advanced Forward** + a pacey
  **Inside Forward**.
- **Win condition:** absorb, win it in your third, hit the **fast break** before
  they reset. Ideal vs aggressive high-pressers.

### C. "Gegenpress" (suffocate them)
- **Build-up:** Balanced/Counter · **Defense:** High/Aggressive press, narrow,
  high line.
- **Formation:** `4-3-3 (4)` or `4-2-2-2`.
- **Key roles:** **Box-to-Box** + **Holding** in midfield, **Pressing** wingers.
- **Win condition:** win the ball in their half and score before they're set.
  **Demands** elite stamina and pace at CB. Switch to plan B when gassed/leading.

### D. "Wing Overload" (stretch & cross)
- **Build-up:** Balanced/Direct · **Defense:** Balanced, normal width.
- **Formation:** `4-2-2-2` or `3-5-2` (wingbacks).
- **Key roles:** **Attacking Wingbacks** + **Inside Forwards** to create 2v1s on
  the touchline; a **Target Forward** + a **Poacher** in the box.
- **Win condition:** overload one flank, whip in **Whipped Pass+** crosses to the
  far post.

### E. "Park the Bus +1" (close out / underdog)
- **Build-up:** Counter · **Defense:** Deep, narrow, lowest sustainable line.
- **Formation:** `5-2-1-2` / `4-5-1`.
- **Key roles:** everyone defends; one **Advanced Forward** to hold the ball up and
  relieve pressure.
- **Win condition:** nick a goal, then defend a lead. Use as your **80th-minute
  preset** to protect a result.

## In-game tactical management (winning the second half)

- **Mentality on the D-pad:** keep a balanced default, bump to attacking when
  chasing, drop to defensive when protecting a lead. Don't leave it on "Ultra
  Attack" — that's how leads evaporate.
- **Make subs ~60' and ~75':** fresh legs change games; fatigue tanks pace,
  passing error, and jockey speed. Bring on pace late.
- **Read their shape:** if they sit deep, widen up and use overlaps + finesse from
  the edge. If they press high, go Counter and play direct behind them.
- **When leading by 2 late:** Deep + Possession build-up to kill the clock with
  short passes in the corners.
