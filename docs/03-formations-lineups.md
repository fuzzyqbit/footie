# 03 — Formations & Sample Lineups

Pick a formation that expresses your identity from `02`, then assign roles from
`04`. Below are the most reliable FC 26 shapes with the strengths, the risk, and a
**sample lineup template** (positions + the Role each slot should run).

> Lineups are written as role assignments, not specific players — slot in your own
> squad. Player-buying advice is in `05`.

---

## 4-2-3-1 — the all-rounder (best starting point)
**Why:** double pivot = defensive insurance; the CAM + three attackers give plenty
going forward. Forgiving and meta-stable.

```
                 ST (Advanced Forward / Poacher)
   LM/LW              CAM                 RM/RW
 (Inside Fwd)   (Playmaker/Shadow)   (Inside Fwd / Winger)
        CDM (Holding)        CDM (Deep-Lying Playmaker)
   LB (Fullback)  CB (Defender)  CB (Defender)  RB (Wingback)
                       GK (Goalkeeper / Sweeper Keeper)
```
- **Strength:** balance; the 10 unlocks low blocks.
- **Risk:** can get outnumbered wide if both fullbacks bomb on.
- **Best for:** Patient Possession, Lethal Counter.

---

## 4-3-3 (variants) — the modern default
Three flavors via the in-game number (4-3-3 (2), (3), (4), False 9, Holding):

- **4-3-3 (4) / Holding:** one CDM screen + two 8s. Balanced, great for press.
- **4-3-3 (False 9):** the ST drops to link play and drag the CB out — elite for
  possession and creating central space for late runners.

```
        LW (Inside Fwd)   ST (False 9)   RW (Inside Fwd)
   CM (Box-to-Box)    CDM (Holding/DLP)    CM (Box-to-Box)
   LB (Wingback)  CB (Defender) CB (Defender) RB (Wingback)
                          GK
```
- **Strength:** central control, pressing triangles, half-space runs.
- **Risk:** the lone CDM is exposed if both 8s push — pick one 8 as **Holding**
  when you attack.
- **Best for:** Gegenpress, Patient Possession.

## 4-2-2-2 — the half-space monster (high ceiling)
**Why:** two narrow CAMs in the half-spaces + two strikers = constant central
overload and devastating give-and-go play. Wingbacks supply the width.

```
            ST (Advanced Fwd)   ST (Poacher)
        CAM (Inside Fwd)        CAM (Shadow Striker)
        CDM (Holding)        CDM (Deep-Lying Playmaker)
   LB (Att. Wingback) CB  CB  RB (Att. Wingback)
                          GK
```
- **Strength:** unreal in the final third; quick 1-2s slice low blocks.
- **Risk:** **the wide channels are yours to cover** — your wingbacks must track
  back or you get countered. Demands stamina.
- **Best for:** Wing Overload, Gegenpress.

---

## 4-4-2 — the defensive bedrock
**Why:** two banks of four are the most solid out-of-possession shape in the game.
Even if you attack from another formation, **dropping into a 4-4-2 block** is the
safest defending shape.

```
        ST (Advanced Fwd)        ST (Target/Poacher)
   LM (Winger)   CM (B2B)   CM (Holding)   RM (Winger)
   LB (Fullback)  CB    CB    RB (Fullback)
                          GK
```
- **Strength:** compact, simple, counters beautifully.
- **Risk:** can get over-run in central midfield 2v3 vs three-man midfields — stay
  compact and don't get dragged out.
- **Best for:** Lethal Counter, learning to defend.

---

## 3-5-2 / 5-2-1-2 — wingback control & game-closing
**Why:** three at the back + wingbacks gives width *and* a back-five safety net.
The same shape doubles as **5-2-1-2** when you tuck the wingbacks in to defend a
lead.

```
            ST (Advanced Fwd)   ST (Poacher)
                   CAM (Shadow Striker)        [5-2-1-2]
   LWB (Att.WB)  CM (B2B)   CM (DLP)   RWB (Att.WB)
        CB (Defender)  CB (Defender)  CB (Defender)
                          GK
```
- **Strength:** numerical security at the back; wingbacks create the width.
- **Risk:** if wingbacks get pinned, you defend with a flat 5 and lose midfield.
- **Best for:** Wing Overload, Park the Bus +1.

## Choosing in 10 seconds
| You want… | Use |
|---|---|
| A safe, do-everything shape | **4-2-3-1** |
| Central control & pressing | **4-3-3 (Holding/False 9)** |
| Maximum attacking output | **4-2-2-2** |
| Rock-solid defending / counters | **4-4-2** |
| Width + a back-line safety net / close out games | **3-5-2 → 5-2-1-2** |

## Set-piece lineup notes
- Put your **best Power Shot / Set Piece Specialist** on direct free kicks; a
  separate **Dead Ball+** taker on the indirect/whipped ones.
- Corners: assign **tall CBs/ST with Aerial+** to attack; keep one fast player back
  to defend the counter.
- Penalties: pick your **highest Composure / Penalty Specialist** — and practice
  the new timing in Skill Games, don't learn it in a final.
