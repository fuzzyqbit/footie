# 06 — Mechanics That Win Games (PS5 buttons)

Tactics get you into good positions; mechanics convert them. These are the skills
that separate wins from losses at every level. Buttons are listed PS5-first
(Xbox in parentheses).

---

## Attacking

### Finishing — your bread and butter
- **Finesse shot:** hold **R1 + ○** (RB + B). The most reliable goal in the game,
  especially with a **Finesse Shot+** player curling far-post from the edge of the
  box. *Learn this first.*
- **Power shot:** hold **L1 + R1 + ○** (LB + RB + B). Huge power, but slow wind-up
  and you're rooted — only use with **time and space** (top of the box, no
  pressure). Lethal from a cutback.
- **Timed finish:** double-tap ○ (second tap as the foot connects) for a green
  accuracy boost. High risk; only when settled.
- **Low driven shot:** **L1 + ○** then release — great in 1v1s vs the keeper.
- **Chip/lob:** **L1 + ○** (LB + B) when the keeper rushes out. Read it early.

### Build-up & passing
- **Through ball:** △ (Y). **Threaded/lobbed through ball:** **L1 + △**.
- **Driven pass:** **R1 + X** (RB + A) — faster, harder to intercept; use to switch
  play and feed feet under pressure.
- **First-time / one-twos:** **L1 + X** (LB + A) sends the receiver on a give-and-go
  run — devastating against low blocks in a 4-2-2-2.
- **Drop pass / heel-to-heel** advanced passes exist for tight combos; learn after
  the basics.

### Dribbling & movement
- **Knock-on / explosive sprint:** **R1** + direction with **L2 (LT) off** for a
  burst past a defender; tap **R1** to push the ball into space.
- **Agile dribbling:** hold **R1** and use the left stick for tight, balanced close
  control — best for **Explosive** dribblers in the box.
- **Protect the ball / shield:** hold **L2 (LT)** with back to defender to shield.
- **Skill moves** worth a low investment for high return:
  - **Ball roll** (hold R-stick to the side) — beat a lunging defender, set up a
    finesse.
  - **La Croqueta** (L1 + R-stick flick) — slip between two defenders in the box.
  - **Heel-to-heel / fake shot** (○ then X, then direction) — change direction at
    speed, create a yard to shoot.
  - Don't over-skill; **two reliable moves** beat a flashy repertoire you fumble.
- **Off-ball runs:** flick the **R-stick** to send a teammate on a run (player-run
  trigger); **L1 + R-stick** to control *who* runs and where. This manufactures the
  runs your tactics set up.

## Defending — where most games are lost

### The priority order (do these, in order)
1. **Switch to the right defender FIRST.** Use **R-stick switching** to grab the
   man nearest the ball *before* you engage. Most goals conceded = controlling the
   wrong player.
2. **Contain, don't dive.** Hold **L2 (LT)** to **jockey/contain** and shepherd the
   attacker away from danger. Stand them up; force them wide or backward.
3. **Use your AI help:** hold **R1 (RB)** for **second-man press** — bring a
   teammate to pressure while *you* hold shape and cover the pass. Don't both
   commit.
4. **Tackle only when sure:** **○ (B)** standing tackle when you can win it cleanly;
   **△ (Y)** for the keeper to rush (sparingly). **Spamming ○ pulls you out of
   position and concedes** — it's the #1 mistake.
5. **Slide tackle** (□ / X) is a **last resort** — last-ditch in the box only; a
   missed slide = a card or a 1v1.

### Pressing without over-committing
- **Don't hold R1 constantly** — your second man over-runs and opens lanes. Pulse
  it.
- **Match their tempo:** if they pass it around the back, **drop and stay
  compact**; don't chase. Win it when they commit forward.
- **Cut the pass, not the player:** position your defender in the **passing lane**
  (the AI Anticipate+/Interceptions PlayStyle helps) and let them run into trouble.

### Defending key situations
- **Through-ball channel:** track the run with the **correct** CB (switch early),
  jockey across, shield the ball out for a goal kick rather than lunging.
- **Crosses:** switch to the CB on the **far post**, hold position, head clear
  (Aerial+ defenders dominate here). Don't ball-watch the near post.
- **Counter-attacks:** **delay** — contain the ball-carrier and retreat to buy time
  for runners to recover, rather than diving in 2v1.

## Goalkeeping (if using Manual/Advanced saves)
- **Manual GK movement** lets you cut the angle in 1v1s — high reward, but a
  mistimed step gifts a chip. Practice in Skill Games before going manual online.

---

## Set pieces
- **Free kicks:** aim with the L-stick, set power ~2 bars for a curling top-corner
  with a Dead Ball+ taker; practice the run-up.
- **Corners:** aim to the **back post** / edge of the six-yard box and trigger a
  late run; tall Aerial+ targets clean up.
- **Penalties:** compose, aim into a corner, use the new timing window — **practice
  in Skill Games**, never improvise in a final.

---

## A 15-minute daily practice routine
1. **Skill Games — Finesse Finishing** (5 min): groove the R1+○ corner.
2. **Defending drill** (5 min): in a friendly, force yourself to **only jockey +
   second-man press**, no manual tackle spamming.
3. **Free kicks & penalties** (5 min): one taker, repeat until the timing is muscle
   memory.

Mechanics are a flywheel — 15 focused minutes a day beats grinding losses online.
